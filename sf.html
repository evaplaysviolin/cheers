San Francisco

Our best estimate for the temperature/weather in San Francisco in late September is that it will be around mid-60's (Fahrenheit), and most likely without rain. As a reminder (even if you're an SF native), please bring something to keep you warm! Lake Merced can get foggy and chilly quickly.

If you're looking for suggestions on sights to see or foods to eat, please take a look below!

Sights to See

  • SF Botanical Garden - Good for a nice, quiet walk, and located inside of Golden Gate Park. Admission is $8 for non-SF residents.
  • Japanese Tea Garden - Also located inside of GG Park, and also great for a walk. It's a beautiful garden and full of photo opportunities! Admission is $8 as well.
  • Twin Peaks (Christmas Tree Point Road) - Visit for stunning views of the city! Bundle up, though, as it gets really windy up there.
  • Lands End Lookout - Get close to the ocean without going to the beach. Check out the Sutro Baths, and if you're up for a hike, the "secret" Lands End Labyrinth.
  • Fort Point - Come here for a view of the Golden Gate Bridge, especially if it's a sunny day.

Foods to Eat

  • Daigo Sushi: Sushi. Our suggestion is the Naruto Roll. Location: Richmond district
  • Nabe: Japanese hot pot. Try anything here! Location: Sunset district
  • Kevin's Noodle House: Pho. Their beef broth is great! Location: Sunset district
  • Sotto Mare: Italian, seafood. Known for their cioppino. Make a reservation, and be prepared for a crowded (but fun) atmosphere. Location: North Beach
  • Lovejoy's Tea Room: High tea. A reservation is necessary, but come here if you like tea, tiny sandwiches, scones to die for, and/or kitschy decoration. Location: Noe Valley
  • Benkyodo: Dessert (manju/mochi). Handmade sweet confections - a must-try is their strawberry mochi, which they only have on the weekends. Location: Japantown
